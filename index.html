About

Hi, I'm Sydney Arlt

I am a junior graphic design and illustration student at Missouri State University. I enjoy making vector based, traditional, and digital illustrations. I am also interested in hand lettering and working with typography

My Recent Work

Jazz Poster

This poster was made for a competition and advanced typography class assignment. I created this illustration in Illustrator. The text ‘Jazz’ and ‘Ruins’ are lettering that I created. I also added a very subtle texture to several aspect of the illustration.

Stereotypes Poster

This poster was created during a workshop with Felipe Taborda in my Advanced Typography class. The goal was to identify a stereotype about your home town and make it into a saying. Then, using basically only typography, create a poster. This poster was created in Illustrator.

French Menu

This French menu design was made for my Advanced Typography class. The goal of this assignment was to create a French inspired menu. This menu was created in Indesign.

Architecture Spread

This architecture magazine spread was created for my class. The goal was to create a spread that accurately reflected the copy that was about Rem Koolhad. This spread was created using Indesign.

Communism Timeline

This timeline was created for my Design Communication and Methodology class. The goal of this assignment was to create a timeline about a given topic, communism under Hitler and Stalin. I had to find similarities and differences between these two regimes and create a timeline that reflected them. This timeline was created using Illustrator.

Vinyl Cover Design

I created this album cover design for my Image Design course. The album is called Pony and it is by Rex Orange County. I picked this design because the album discusses themes of growth after a great deal of struggle. Thus, my illustration represnts growth happening even when you feel you are at your lowest.

Logo Design

This is a logo design done for Rivercity Ironwork. The owner wanted a logo design that represented the location of the company and what they provide. Thus, I went with a fleur de lis, which is a symbol commonly used in the St. Louis area, and combined it with a wrench, braided steel, and nuts.
